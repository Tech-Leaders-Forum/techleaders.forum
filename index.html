Next Forum Session

Should Agent Smith conduct your performance reviews?

August 2026 · Budapest

As AI tools quietly enter more and more workplace conversations, feedback is becoming one of the next frontiers to rethink. Can AI help leaders prepare, structure, or deliver better feedback — or does outsourcing difficult conversations risk weakening trust, empathy, and human judgment? In this session, we’ll explore where AI can genuinely support feedback cultures, where it should stay far away, and why honest human conversations still matter more than ever. Expect real-world leadership dilemmas, uncomfortable questions, and practical insights from people navigating these tensions in their daily work. Because the real challenge may not be whether AI can give feedback — but whether leaders are slowly forgetting how to do it themselves.

What happens here

A small group from the local ecosystem. Different contexts, similar responsibilities.

Each session starts with a panel: people who have actually been in the situations we talk about. Then we open it up: questions can get more practical, answers less certain, and at some point the conversation might become more interesting than the panel itself.

How it works

  • Panel discussion: real stories, not theory
  • Open conversation: challenge, question, expand
  • Informal networking afterwards: continue the discussion, make new connections

House rules

  • What's shared here stays here
  • Challenge ideas, not people
  • No pitching, no selling
  • Leave space for others
